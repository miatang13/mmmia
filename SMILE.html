Project Background

What & Why?

I started volunteering at PALS in 2016, a specialized school for kindergarten children to grade 12 (age 17) young adults who are on the spectrum.

As a volunteer,

My daily tasks mainly revolves around preparing teaching material for the teachers, which gives me full access to what materials they need for teaching.

After cutting, folding, and laminating paper for months,
I want to make a change.

Realization

Mundane tasks lead to discovery of design opportunity

By having the day-to-day observations, I realized how the majority of the physical materials which needed to be made and remade numerous times, can be transformed into digital interactions that would allow the same learning outcome.

Learning about existing teaching methods

Physical Exercises to Digital Interactions

PALS implements Applied Behavior Analysis, which uses Antecedent, Behavior and Consequence to help understand behaviors.

Thanks to PALS's great support throughout the project, I had direct access to teaching materials that are in use.

Thinking of innovative exercises

Being able to write is a huge step for people on the spectrum, hence I want to introduce an exercise that both trains hand-eye coordination as well as letter recognition.
