GreenMellon Visuals Analog About Resume

This Green Mellon project has 2 parts: Systems Mapping and User Experience Design.
As a group, my teammates and I dived into the wicked problem, Waste Management in Pittsburgh, and proposed a digital intervention to solve the issue.





The Systems Map was exhibited at RSD8 Systems Thinking Conference.

Service Design | CMU Design Spring 2019

Duration: 3 Weeks
Skills: Systems Thinking, Information Architecture, UI / UX Design

Collaborators: Joseph Zhang, Youie Cho, Sammie Kim, Elizabeth Han, Charmaine Qiu

Part A /
Understanding Waste Management Through Systems Mapping

Understanding, visualizing and intervening in complex large-scale, socio-technical systems.









/ Future Projection Map
/ Mass Map

Initial Mappings: Collective Brainstorming

At the beginning phase of the project, we engaged in face-to-face discussions with our team members, collaboratively came to a thorough and multi-perspective understanding of the waste management system in the local Pittsburgh area. We communicated and merged our ideas through physical post-it and venn-diagrams to work as a team efficiently.






Mapping using Kumu

Secondary Mapping: Digital Collaboration

As our understanding furthered and idea developed, we switched to using an online platform to work toward our final digital product. In this project, we used Kumu to layer complex information in top of our structure mapping.

Final Mapping

To present our thorough understanding of the existing system, we divded information into 6 categories based on the waste's original location.

Part B /
Designing Intervention: The Green Mellon Value System

After understanding the problem thoroughly, we started designing a solution. Approaching the issue with a hollistic mindset and understanding the lack of a "perfect solution" for wicked problems like waste management, my group and I came up with a system of interventions that is physical, virtual, and interactive.
